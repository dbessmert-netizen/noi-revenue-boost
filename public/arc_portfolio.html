How the program works — five steps
1
NOI builds and finances one shared system per building
NOI designs, finances, installs and maintains a shared rooftop solar array and battery on each building — sized to the roof and the community's load. ARC writes no upfront cheque. On multifamily, a single larger shared system per building brings the installed cost well below single-family pricing — indicatively $10,000–$13,000 per home versus ~$19,000 for detached homes.
2
ARC leases the equipment — a fixed monthly fee, not an energy charge
ARC enters an equipment lease & solar-services agreement and pays NOI a fixed monthly lease fee for access to and operation of the system. The fee is not tied to energy production or consumption, and no electricity is sold under the agreement.
3
Because ARC owns the equipment, the tax credits flow to ARC
As the tax owner of the equipment, ARC captures the 30% federal ITC plus the 10% domestic-content bonus (40% total) through US-manufactured EcoFlow hardware, plus accelerated MACRS depreciation. Applied against the lease, these credits sharply reduce ARC's real monthly cost.
4
Residents opt into a fixed solar-equipment amenity fee
The shared system feeds each building behind the meter, lowering residents' grid draw. Residents who opt in pay a fixed monthly solar-equipment access fee as part of the amenity package — set below the utility savings the system delivers, so they come out ahead. The fee is for access to on-site equipment, not a charge for electricity.
5
ARC keeps the spread — new NOI plus a more valuable asset
ARC keeps the difference between the resident amenity fees and the fixed equipment lease. That recurring income lifts NOI and, capitalized, the property's value — a real advantage on any future sale or refinance. NOI handles monitoring, maintenance and optional billing end-to-end.

The structure, in one line
This is an equipment lease and solar-services agreement for shared solar and battery equipment, with a fixed monthly fee for access to and operation of the system. No electricity is sold, and no payment is tied to energy output or consumption. ARC is the tax owner of the equipment and receives the ITC and depreciation; residents pay an optional fixed solar-equipment amenity fee. NOI funds, installs, maintains and monitors — no upfront cheque, zero operational burden.
Compliance & structure

NOI is not a public utility and does not sell electricity. All payments under the program are fixed monthly fees for access to, and operation and maintenance of, on-site solar equipment — not per-kWh energy charges, energy fees, or utility replacement.

The arrangement is structured as an equipment lease and solar-services agreement. ARC is treated as the tax owner of the equipment and receives all associated tax benefits. The resident-facing charge is a fixed solar-equipment amenity fee, provided as part of the property's amenity package, unrelated to electricity usage or production. This structure is designed for compliance in PPA-restricted markets including Alabama and Georgia.
